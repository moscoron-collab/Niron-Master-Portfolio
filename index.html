📖 Monthly Guide — what to do when a new month comes

⚙️ What happens automatically vs. what you do
The 4 Niron LLCs (Divando, Dorado, Yale, Donald) are pulled from AppFolio automatically every day from the 15th to the 25th — you don't push any button for those. Everything else on this page (the 3 out-of-state properties, repairs, distributions, property tax, and the bank-true cash) is entered by hand. This guide is that hand-entry checklist, in the order to do it.
🟡 Do this first, every time: set “Signed in as” (top-right) to who you are — every entry is stamped with your name, and the page blocks saving until it's set. Then pick the new month in the month dropdown (top-left). It jumps to the newest month with data on its own once AppFolio lands.
  1. Confirm the AppFolio pull landed  (around the 16th–26th)
    In the month dropdown (top-left), select the new month. If the LLC cards (Divando, Dorado, Yale, Donald) show numbers for that month, the automatic pull worked — nothing to do for the 4 LLCs.
    If a month is still blank by the ~26th, the AppFolio login likely needs attention — tell whoever maintains the automation; the rest of the steps below can still be done.
  2. Enter the 3 out-of-state statements — click the 📋 button (bottom-right)
    These 3 are not on AppFolio and must be typed in each month: 8222 Hare Ave (Jacksonville, Suncoast), 3899 Joest Rd & 6580 Stockport Dr (Memphis, Mid South). Enter the amount that actually hit the bank (the deposited amount), not the statement's stated NOI — they can differ. The form shows “X of 3 entered” and marks each ✅ done / ⬜ not yet. They roll up under Divando automatically.
  3. Enter repair / maintenance invoices — click the 🔧 button (bottom-right)
    For each invoice: date, property (the LLC auto-fills), category, amount, description. Set “Paid By” (Debit Card = instant draw · Check = mailed, drafts ~1 wk · Sent to CPA), tick Paid if it's already paid, and attach the invoice file if you have it. Anything marked “Sent to CPA / unpaid” shows up in the 🧾 CPA Invoices list for the accountant.
  4. Record partner distributions — click the 💰 button (bottom-right)
    When money is taken out, enter the month and the amount for Ron and for Nir (Dorado also includes Simon). This feeds the Your Distribution card and the Partner Distributions section. Policy is an equal split, so the two amounts usually match.
  5. Property tax — only when you actually pay a bill — the 🧾 Property Tax tab
    When you pay a parcel's tax online (Divando & Dorado), open the Property Tax tab, click ✏️ on that row, and record the paid date · amount · confirmation #. The balance and the red “Property Tax Owed” tile update automatically. Donald & Yale are escrow (the lender pays) — nothing to do for them.
  6. Import bank statements for true cash — click 📥 Import Bank (top)
    Download each LLC operating account's monthly transaction CSV and select all four at once. This builds the “True Cash — Bank-Verified” section — the real net after tax, utilities, accountant and repair checks that the AppFolio number alone doesn't capture. Nothing is uploaded; it stays in this browser.
  7. Verify — glance at the 🩺 Run Audit chip
    Green or amber is fine (amber is the known insurance note, not an error). Then eyeball the LLC cards and Net Cashflow for the new month to make sure nothing looks off. Done. ✅
💬 Stuck on a number or a property? Use the 💬 Ask Claude button (bottom-right) — it knows the whole Niron portfolio.  |  Order matters most for step 1 (AppFolio is the base); steps 2–6 can be done in any order.

📥 Import Bank Statements

Pick the monthly transaction CSV export for each LLC operating account — you can select all four at once. The dashboard reads them in your browser (nothing is uploaded), figures out each LLC automatically, and computes the true net cash = income minus every real expense it sees (mortgage, SBA, insurance, property tax, city utilities, accountant, repairs). Distributions are tracked per partner (Ron / Nir / Simon). It stores the result in this browser, so it sticks across refreshes.
